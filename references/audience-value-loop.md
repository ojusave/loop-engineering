# Audience-Value and Knowledge-Advantage Loop

## Outcome

Ensure an audience-facing artifact earns attention by delivering a credible reader or listener delta, not merely polished packaging of common knowledge.

Use this loop for conference titles and abstracts, talks, essays, thought leadership, launch narratives, landing-page arguments, executive presentations, and other public work whose success depends on why this author or organization is worth hearing.

## Establish the commodity baseline

Before drafting, record:

- intended audience and the decision, belief, or capability the artifact should change
- what a competent generalist could already produce from common knowledge and the supplied public context
- what the audience probably believes before encountering the artifact
- what they should know, see, or be able to do afterward
- why this author, speaker, team, or artifact has access to that delta

The baseline is not a literal claim that a model can never reproduce the final words. It is a test of whether the artifact depends on evidence, access, synthesis, or judgment beyond generic domain knowledge.

## Identify the knowledge advantage

A valid advantage may come from:

- original measurements, telemetry, reporting, or research
- firsthand implementation, operations, incidents, or failure analysis
- a reproducible experiment, demonstration, benchmark, or artifact
- a new model or taxonomy derived from observable evidence
- a synthesis that resolves a real contradiction across credible sources
- access to a consequential decision, constraint, tradeoff, or current bet
- unusually clear instruction that enables a difficult task, even when the underlying facts are public

Proprietary data is not mandatory. Novel wording is not an advantage. A provocative opinion without evidence is not an advantage.

Do not invent internal data, personal experience, customer stories, access, findings, or organizational bets. If the necessary advantage may exist but is not available, return to the parent with the exact evidence or author input needed.

## Define the audience delta

Write one sentence for each:

```text
Commodity baseline:
Author's evidence or access:
Audience delta:
Practical consequence:
```

Reject a delta that only promises awareness, inspiration, best practices, a checklist, or a broad overview unless that is explicitly the audience's unmet need.

For a public description or abstract, name enough of the mechanism, finding, method, or tension to prove there is substance. Do not turn the abstract into an empty teaser. Do not reveal every result when discovery is part of the experience.

## Substitution test

Perform a cold comparison against the commodity baseline:

1. Remove the author name, company, adjectives, and polished phrasing.
2. List the artifact's load-bearing takeaways or promises.
3. Ask which takeaways require the author's evidence, access, artifact, or judgment.
4. Check that the claimed advantage will actually appear in the final work.
5. Mark any unsupported advantage as a delivery risk, not a copy improvement.

If every takeaway survives unchanged without the author's particular access or work, the artifact is substitutable. Route back to research, strategy, or the author before further polishing.

Do not use the substitution test to demand novelty from routine documentation, transactional copy, safety instructions, or straightforward explanations. In those cases, accuracy and task completion may be the full value.

## Public-description check

For titles, abstracts, and promotional descriptions, verify:

- the title exposes the tension, claim, or object of inquiry rather than only naming the topic
- the description identifies the audience-relevant problem
- at least one concrete evidence form is named, such as traces, measurements, incidents, experiments, implementation details, or a live artifact
- the speaker or author's authority is connected to that evidence rather than presented as biography alone
- the promised takeaway changes a model, decision, or capability
- curiosity comes from a real unresolved result or consequence, not withheld basics
- the artifact does not promise findings the underlying work cannot support or disclose

## Return criteria

Return `PASS` when the commodity baseline, knowledge advantage, audience delta, and practical consequence are explicit; the advantage is supported and deliverable; and representative evidence shows the public copy makes that value legible. Report `OUTCOME VALIDATED` only when a target audience can accurately identify the promised delta; otherwise report `ARTIFACT VERIFIED` and name the pending audience test.

Return `REWORK_REQUIRED` when the artifact is polished but substitutable, the title hides the actual claim, or the promised takeaway remains generic.
Return `BLOCKED` when the needed evidence, access, disclosure approval, or author input is unavailable.
Return `NEEDS_HUMAN_JUDGMENT` when multiple evidence-backed deltas remain and the choice depends on positioning, taste, or disclosure risk.
