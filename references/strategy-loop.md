# Strategy Loop

## Outcome

Make the named decision owner decision-ready with a recommendation that follows from evidence, constraints, tradeoffs, feasibility, and likely consequences.

## Establish the decision

Record:

- decision owner
- decision to make
- objective
- current situation
- constraints
- time horizon
- stakeholders
- irreversible choices
- what success would look like
- what is outside scope
- the commodity baseline when the strategy or recommendation must be distinctive to this organization

Do not create strategy without a decision.

## Ground the situation

Use repository, company, market, customer, technical, or operational evidence available in scope.

When facts may be current, invoke the research loop and source-integrity audit. Treat vendor, marketing, affiliate, PR, SEO, AEO, and GEO content as incentive-shaped evidence rather than neutral validation.

Distinguish:

- observed condition
- assumption
- interpretation
- forecast
- publisher incentive and independence

For public strategy, positioning, or thought leadership, also identify the contextual advantage: which recommendation changes because of the organization's constraints, data, capabilities, customers, failures, or risk posture. A recommendation that remains unchanged after removing all task-specific context is a generic baseline, not a finished strategy.

## Generate real alternatives

Create materially different choices, including a credible `do less` or `do nothing yet` option when appropriate.

Do not produce three wording variations of one recommendation.

For each option, assess:

- expected upside
- cost and complexity
- dependencies
- reversibility
- time to evidence
- failure mode
- opportunity cost
- second-order effect
- fit with constraints

## Test the recommendation

Attack the provisional recommendation from at least these perspectives when relevant:

- user or customer
- operator or maintainer
- budget owner
- security or compliance owner
- skeptical expert
- future migration owner

Ask:

- What assumption must be true?
- What evidence contradicts it?
- What happens if adoption is slower?
- What can be tested cheaply first?
- What would cause regret?
- What is the smallest reversible commitment?

## Feasibility

Translate the recommendation into:

- first action
- evidence milestone
- owner or decision point, only when known
- required resources
- cut line
- stop or pivot condition

Do not recommend an effort whose preparation, maintenance, or coordination cost is ignored.

## Return criteria

Return `PASS` and `DECISION READY` when one option clearly best fits the evidence and constraints, the decision owner can act, assumptions and reversal conditions are explicit, and a feasible next test exists. When distinctiveness matters, the recommendation must also show which task-specific evidence or constraint changes it from the commodity baseline.

Return `REWORK_REQUIRED` when alternatives or consequences are underdeveloped.
Return `BLOCKED` when key evidence is unavailable.
Return `NEEDS_HUMAN_JUDGMENT` when the decision is primarily a values, taste, budget, or risk choice.
